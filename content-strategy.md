## Voice & Style Guide

### Do:
- Lead with strong opinions, then back with evidence
- Share real numbers (costs, time saved, bugs caught)
- Name competitors/incumbents when making points
- Use "I" and "we" ‚Äî personal experience trumps theory
- Include code snippets, architecture diagrams, actual prompts
- End with actionable takeaways

### Don't:
- Hedge with "it depends" without saying what it depends on
- Use consulting-speak ("leverage," "synergies," "digital transformation")
- Write vendor content masquerading as thought leadership
- Avoid controversy ‚Äî strong opinions get engagement

### Tone Keywords:
- **Direct** ‚Äî No throat-clearing, get to the point
- **Opinionated** ‚Äî Take positions, don't just present options
- **Technical** ‚Äî Code and architecture, not just strategy
- **Practical** ‚Äî What actually works, not what should work
- **Human** ‚Äî Admit failures, share learning moments
